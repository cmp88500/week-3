A Stress Test

  • Include several questions.
    • One that lists warning signs of being negatively affected by stress (loss of sleep, overwhelmed, etc)
    • One that lists health symptoms
    • Another that lists recommended coping methods.
  • Then, based on user answers, provide some guidance: For instance, if a user reports 4 stress-related symptoms, and 6 warning signs apply to them, suggest some resources to help them out.
  • Or, if a user says 4 warning signs of high stress apply to them, but they also report using 5 of the recommended ways to manage stress, tell them they're doing a pretty good job.
  • Include at least 3 possible results.

So it looks like we will have three groups of checkboxes: one for warning signs, one for health symptoms, and one for coping methods. Scores will be tallied for each group, and certain scores would lead to certain outcomes.

My partner and I decided to make three divs for each group with unique IDs for jQuery purposes (#group1, #group2, #group3), and identical classes for CSS styling (.group). All three groups would be wrapped within a form tag with ID (#formOne), given a paragraph to label each group, and 12 checkboxes each, all with same name(input-name1, input-name2, input-name3), and all the checkboxes will have unique values. After the last div is closed out, a button will close it out before we wrap up the form. We will also need a place to display our outcomes, so a paragraph (#response) is added after the form. Last, but not least, you should run a branching statement. In the exercise, they want you to have alerts for three different outcomes:

  • One for having at least 6 warning symptoms and 4 stress related symptoms, with less than five stress relief activities practiced.
  • One for having the symptoms above, but with practicing five or more activities
  • One for fewer symptoms in general
